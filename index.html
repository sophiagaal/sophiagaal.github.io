Bio

I am a senior undergraduate student at Colorado College pursuing a B.A. in Comprehensive Physics. My research focuses on geophysical applications, particularly paleomagnetic analysis and ice sheet dynamics. As a Research Experience for Undergraduates (REU) participant at Stanford University, I investigated meteorites as paleomagnetic recorders, conducting laboratory experiments including stepwise demagnetization and magnetic hysteresis measurements. I've also researched Antarctic ice sheet retreat patterns at The University of Arizona through diatom and IRD records.

My academic interests include Electromagnetic Theory, Geophysics, and Climate Science. Specifically, I focus on:

  • Exploring electromagnetic wave propagation through ice for glaciology and climate research applications
  • Applying paleomagnetic analysis techniques to understand planetary formation processes
  • Developing technical skills in laboratory instrumentation and scientific computing
