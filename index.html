About
Hello! I am a senior undergraduate student at Colorado College, double majoring in Computer Science and Mathematics. My primary research interests lie at the intersection of Machine Learning, Natural Language Processing (NLP), and Formal Methods.

I am currently completing my thesis under Professor Robert Rennie, where I explore autoformalization by leveraging fine-tuned neural models to translate informal mathematical arguments into formal proofs. Additionally, I have worked as a Research Fellow on the State of the Rockies Bears Ears Project, applying NLP techniques to preserve Indigenous oral histories and link them to environmental data for conservation strategies.

Previously, I interned at SocialTechLabs, Inc., where I developed features for a mental wellness mobile app using SwiftUI and optimized backend processes with Go and AWS, improving efficiency and stability.

My current research interests include:
  • Advancing LLM Evaluation frameworks to improve autoformalization, bridging neural models with symbolic reasoning.
  • Developing AI-driven models for cultural preservation and community-focused conservation initiatives.
I am always excited to collaborate on projects at the intersection of AI, NLP, and societal impact. Please feel free to reach out if you’d like to discuss research opportunities, PhD applications, or mentorship!
